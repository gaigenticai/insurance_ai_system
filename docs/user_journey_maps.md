# User Journey Maps for Configurable Insurance Platform

This document outlines user journey maps for key personas interacting with the configurable insurance platform.

---

## User Journey Map 1: Sarah - Claims Processor

*   **Persona:** Sarah - Claims Processor
*   **Goal:** Process a new 'Auto Windshield Repair' claim from FNOL to either auto-approval or escalation efficiently and accurately according to the institution's configured workflow and rules.

| Phases/Major Stages      | User Actions                                                                                                | System Touchpoints/Screens                                                                                                                               | User Thoughts/Feelings (Optional)                                                                     | Opportunities                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| :----------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1. Claim Intake (FNOL Review)** | 1. Receives notification of a new claim or selects a new claim from the "Unassigned Claims" queue/list. <br> 2. Opens the claim to view initial details. <br> 3. Reviews FNOL information submitted by the claimant (customer name, policy #, incident date, description, photos if any). | - Dashboard Notifications Widget <br> - Claims Queue/List View <br> - Claim Detail View (Summary/FNOL Tab) <br> - Embedded Document Viewer                                | "Okay, a new windshield claim. Let's see the basics." <br> "Hope all the initial info is here."                                                                             | - Clear, prioritized notification system. <br> - At-a-glance summary of FNOL data on the claim dashboard or list view. <br> - Visual indicators for missing required FNOL fields directly on the claim summary.                                                                                                              |
| **2. Initial Validation & Policy Verification** | 1. Checks for any immediate system flags or alerts (e.g., duplicate claim, policy inactive at Date of Loss). <br> 2. System automatically displays policy validity and relevant coverage information (e.g., "Windshield Coverage: Active"). <br> 3. Sarah reviews this system-provided information. | - Claim Detail View (System Flags/Alerts Area) <br> - Policy Snapshot/Widget (within Claim View) <br> - Coverage Details Section (dynamically populated) <br> - System-triggered validation messages/indicators | "Is the policy active for this date? Is windshield damage specifically covered?" <br> "System says coverage is active and applicable. Good, less manual checking for me." | - Prominent, color-coded display of system-generated validation flags (policy status, basic coverage check results from the workflow engine). <br> - Direct link to the full policy details if deeper investigation is needed. <br> - Clear indication if the claim type matches a defined coverage on the policy. |
| **3. Reviewing Custom Fields & Documents** | 1. Navigates to or scrolls to the section displaying claim-specific custom fields for "Auto Windshield Repair" (e.g., "Point of Impact," "Repair or Replace?"). <br> 2. Verifies completeness of these fields. <br> 3. Checks the document management section for required documents (e.g., "Photo of Damage," "Repair Estimate") as defined in the claim type configuration. <br> 4. Opens and reviews uploaded photos/estimates. | - Claim Detail View (Custom Fields Section - dynamically rendered based on claim type) <br> - Document Management Component (shows required vs. uploaded, with previews/links) <br> - Integrated Document/Image Viewer (with zoom, rotate) | "Okay, this is a 'Windshield Repair' type, so I should see fields like 'Point of Impact'." <br> "Are the photos clear? Does the estimate look reasonable?" | - Custom fields section dynamically rendered and clearly labeled based on claim type configuration. <br> - Visual checklist for required documents, with clear status (Uploaded, Missing, Pending AI Review). <br> - High-quality, fast-loading image viewer with basic annotation tools. |
| **4. AI-Assisted Assessment** | 1. Reviews AI-generated insights if configured for this claim type (e.g., damage assessment from photo analysis: severity, estimated cost range). <br> 2. Notes the AI-generated fraud score and any key factors highlighted by the system. | - Claim Detail View (AI Insights Panel / Automated Assessment Section) <br> - Fraud Score Widget with drill-down for reasons.                                   | "The AI damage assessment suggests a repair is possible and estimates $X. The uploaded estimate is for $Y." <br> "Fraud score is low, that's reassuring." <br> "Why is the AI suggesting this fraud score?" | - Clear, concise presentation of AI outputs (e.g., damage type, cost range, fraud likelihood). <br> - Explainable AI: Show top 2-3 factors contributing to a fraud score or assessment. <br> - Side-by-side comparison tool for AI estimate vs. uploaded repair estimate if discrepancies are flagged. |
| **5. Rule Evaluation & Decision Support** | 1. System automatically evaluates configured business rules (e.g., settlement limits for fast-track, specific fraud flags for this claim type). <br> 2. Sarah reviews the system's recommended action (e.g., "Auto-Approve," "Escalate - Exceeds Auto-Approval Threshold," "Refer to SIU - High Fraud Indicators"). <br> 3. Understands the reasons for the recommendation based on rule outcomes displayed. | - Claim Detail View (System Recommendation Area / Rules Engine Log/Summary) <br> - Action Buttons reflecting possible next steps (e.g., "Approve," "Deny," "Escalate") | "The system suggests 'Auto-Approve' because the amount is within the configured limit for windshields, and no fraud flags were triggered." <br> "Or, it's flagging it for review because the custom field 'Previous Windshield Claims (Last 12m)' is 3." | - Clear, human-readable summary of which key rules passed/failed and their impact on the recommendation. <br> - Highlighting of specific data points (standard or custom) that triggered certain rules. <br> - "What-if" capability (simple version) to see how changing a minor data point might affect rule outcomes (for understanding). |
| **6. Action & Finalization** | **If Auto-Approval Path:** <br> 1. Reviews the auto-calculated settlement amount. <br> 2. Adds an optional closing note. <br> 3. Clicks "Confirm Auto-Approval." <br><br> **If Escalation Path:** <br> 1. Selects an escalation reason from a pre-defined list (if applicable based on rules) or types a custom reason. <br> 2. Adds detailed notes for the next reviewer. <br> 3. Clicks "Escalate Claim." | - Claim Finalization Screen (for approval) / Payment Details Section <br> - Claim Escalation Form/Modal (for escalation) <br> - Claim History/Audit Trail <br> - Work Queue update (claim moves to new queue/assignee) | **Auto-Approval:** "Great, this is straightforward. Done." <br> **Escalation:** "Okay, this needs a specialist. My notes should give them all the context." | - One-click "Confirm Auto-Approval" when all conditions met. <br> - Pre-populated escalation reasons based on rule triggers. <br> - Easy selection of escalation path/team if multiple are configured. <br> - Automated notifications (internal and to claimant if configured) upon status change. |
